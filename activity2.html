Activity 2: Debate Pong

Instructions:

  • Set up two teams on opposite sides of a long table (3–6 students per team).
  • Place 6–10 cups in a triangle or line on each side with a card hidden under each.
  • Each card should contain a provocative online statement labeled as either Fact, Bluff, or Fuel.
  • Teams take turns throwing ping pong balls. When a cup is hit, the card is revealed and must be read aloud.
  • The receiving team must debate and decide the card's classification and justify their answer.
  • Points are awarded for accurate identification or strong, reasonable arguments.

Sample Cards

Statement:

"If someone doesn't reply to your message within five minutes, they’re ignoring you on purpose."

Correct Type: Fuel – emotionally charged and manipulative.

Statement:

"More than 60% of teens report feeling pressure to look a certain way on social media."

Correct Type: Fact – based on research or survey data.

Statement:

"All influencers only care about likes and money."

Correct Type: Bluff – overgeneralization with no strong basis.

Debrief Prompts

  • Which cards were the hardest to categorize?
  • Did you disagree with your teammates? Why?
  • What emotions did some of these statements trigger?
  • How could we train ourselves to pause and reflect before reacting online?
