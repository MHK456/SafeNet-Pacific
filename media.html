Media Literacy Toolkit

What is Media Literacy?

Media literacy is the ability to access, analyse, evaluate, create, and act using all forms of communication. It helps people become critical thinkers, effective communicators, and active digital citizens. In the age of social media, understanding how to question what we see, hear, and read online is more important than ever.

Conviction Bias

Conviction bias is when we believe something more strongly simply because we feel strongly about it—even when we lack real evidence. The louder or more passionate we feel, the more convinced we are that we're right.

Confirmation Bias

Confirmation bias is when we only pay attention to information that supports what we already believe, and ignore or reject anything that contradicts it. This creates an echo chamber where our beliefs are constantly reinforced.

Group Bias

Group bias (or in-group bias) is when we favour information, opinions, or people who belong to the same group as us—whether it's our culture, gender, nationality, or friend circle—while dismissing others.
