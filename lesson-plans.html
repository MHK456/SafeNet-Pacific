Interactive Workshop Activities

Welcome to the SafeNet Pacific workshop activity hub. These interactive sessions are designed to build digital awareness, media literacy, and reflective habits among youth. Explore the detailed guides and launch the activities below.

Activity 1: Algorithm Detective

Overview: Students are shown a mock "For You" page resembling a TikTok or Instagram feed. The posts appear random, but each feed is designed to reflect a user profile shaped by their previous interactions. Students must analyse the feed to deduce the kind of content the user engaged with and what the algorithm has learned about them.

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand how algorithms personalise content based on engagement
  • Recognise how repeated behaviors (likes, scrolls, views) influence visibility
  • Identify patterns and messaging biases in feed content
  • Critically reflect on their own media exposure and digital habits

Materials Needed:

  • Printed or digital mock social media feeds (4 types: motivational, conspiracy, fashion/selfies, mental health)
  • Butcher paper or whiteboards
  • Pens/markers
  • Optional: group worksheet templates

Instructions:

  1. Divide students into groups of 3–5 and give each a different mock feed.
  2. Ask each group to study the content and answer: What patterns do you notice? What kind of user is this? What has the algorithm learned?
  3. Groups present findings and compare results across feeds.
  4. Wrap up with a speed debrief: How can you take control of your algorithm?

Activity 2: Debate Pong

Overview: Students face off in teams in a pong-style game where each cup contains a card with a provocative online statement. When a ball lands in a cup, the team must classify the statement as Fact, Bluff, or Fuel—and defend their reasoning in a short debate. This promotes deeper understanding of digital rhetoric and emotional manipulation in media.

Learning Objectives:

  • Develop awareness of manipulative media tactics
  • Enhance skills in critical reasoning and media debate
  • Learn to pause and analyse before reacting to posts
  • Build confidence in articulating and defending opinions

Materials Needed:

  • Plastic cups (6–10 per team)
  • Ping pong balls
  • Statement cards labeled: Fact, Bluff, Fuel (with statements attached underneath)
  • Whiteboard or scoreboard for tracking points

Instructions:

  1. Divide students into two teams and set up cups pong-style.
  2. Teams take turns trying to land balls in opposing team’s cups.
  3. When a cup is hit, the receiving team reads the hidden card aloud and classifies it (Fact/Bluff/Fuel).
  4. If correct, they earn a point. If wrong, the other team may challenge or explain.
  5. After all cups are emptied, hold a debrief discussion: What was surprising? What made something sound convincing?
