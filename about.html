What is Technology-Facilitated Gender-Based Violence (TFGBV)?

Technology-Facilitated Gender-Based Violence (TFGBV) refers to any act of abuse, harassment, or harm carried out using digital technologies—such as mobile phones, social media, messaging apps, GPS, surveillance devices, and more. These tools are used by perpetrators to threaten, stalk, intimidate, control, or silence victims, often targeting women and girls.

In the Pacific, as more people gain access to the internet and digital devices, TFGBV is rising rapidly. Cultural taboos, stigma, and lack of legal protections further amplify the harm. Many women and girls face online harassment, image-based abuse, doxxing, impersonation, or even the misuse of AI-generated content like deepfakes to degrade or blackmail them.

The Urgency in the Pacific

  • Up to 88% of women in Asia-Pacific reported online violence in 2020
  • Adolescent girls are particularly vulnerable due to limited digital literacy and support
  • AI technologies (e.g., deepfake pornography, chatbots, impersonation scams) add new layers of risk
  • Lack of culturally relevant prevention frameworks or reporting systems in island nations

TFGBV is not just about technology—it’s about power. It reflects and reinforces offline inequalities, taking new forms in digital spaces. That’s why SafeNet Pacific and Team Ladybug are committed to addressing this issue with Pacific-led, community-informed strategies.
